My Roadmap to Design Success

Becoming a designer doesn't happen overnight. It takes learning, practicing, reflecting, and perseverance. Here's the step-by-step plan I've laid out to reach my dream:

Complete Secondary Education and Explore Interests

Participate in art competitions, design clubs, and explore various forms of visual expression to discover what I love most.

Pursue a Design-Related Diploma or Degree

Target schools: The One Academy, Limkokwing, UTAR, MMU, UiTM, UM. Courses: Graphic Design, Multimedia Arts, Visual Communication, etc.

Master Essential Design Tools

Must-learn software: Photoshop, Illustrator, InDesign, Figma, After Effects, Canva.

Build a Strong Design Portfolio

Curate my best works in a digital portfolio using platforms like Behance, personal websites, or PDF decks to showcase versatility and creativity.

Join Internships, Competitions, and Freelance Projects

Gain real-world experience in advertising agencies, branding studios, printing firms, or UI/UX teams.

Engage in Design Communities and Networking

Join online design challenges, attend meetups, visit exhibitions, and follow creative forums to meet other designers and stay updated.

Keep Learning and Expanding My Skills

Learn new areas like motion graphics, web design, interactive media, and digital branding.

Build My Personal Brand and Online Presence

Create design-focused social media accounts (IG, LinkedIn, Behance) and regularly share my creative work.

Enter the Workforce and Gain Experience

Start as a Junior Designer → Move to Mid-Level → Senior Designer → Art Director. I might also consider becoming a freelancer or starting my own design brand.

Keep Growing and Innovating for Life

Stay curious about the world, sharpen my skills, collaborate with other industries, and use design to make a real impact.
